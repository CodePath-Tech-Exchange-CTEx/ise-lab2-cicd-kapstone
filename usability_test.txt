# Usability Test

## Intro
Hi, my name is Johnny and we are a team of 4 students building a fitness social media app called SDS. Today we'd like you to help us test two new features we're designing. You will be playing the role of a fitness enthusiast who uses the app regularly.

Your job is not to impress us — there are no right or wrong answers. We want to see how you naturally interact with the design. Please think out loud as much as possible — tell us what you're looking at, what you're thinking, and what you expect to happen before you do it.

We will be showing you the live app and asking you to interact with it directly.

---

## Tasks

### Feature 1: Meal Plan

Scenario: You just finished a workout and you want to plan out your meals for the week to help you hit your fitness goals. Please show us how you would do that using this app.

### Feature 2: Goal Setter

Scenario: You want to set a new fitness goal for yourself and track your progress toward it. Please show us how you would do that using this app.

---

## Notes

### Participant 1
- Feature 1 observations: Completed meal plan task smoothly, no hesitation
- Feature 2 observations: Completed goal setter task smoothly, no hesitation
- General: Said the app was smooth, images loaded fine, no long load times

### Participant 2
- Feature 1 observations: Completed task but felt the UI could be clearer
- Feature 2 observations: Completed task but wanted more guidance on what to do
- General: Rated app 6/10, liked the UI design but felt the app could better guide users on what they're looking at

## Results

### Issues Found

#### Meal Plan Issues

**Issue 1:**
- Issue: Users were unsure what the dropdown arrow (▽) on each meal 
  section does before clicking it
- Hypothesis: The arrow icon alone is not a clear enough signal that 
  clicking it expands the meal details. Users expected a label or 
  tooltip explaining the action
- Remedy: Add a small text label next to the arrow such as 
  "tap to expand" or replace the arrow with a clearly labeled 
  "View Details" button

**Issue 2:**
- Issue: The calorie goal field shows "####" as a placeholder which 
  does not tell users what number to enter or what the goal refers to
- Hypothesis: Users do not know if the goal is daily, weekly, or 
  per meal, causing confusion about what number to type in
- Remedy: Add a helper text below the goal field such as 
  "Enter your daily calorie target (e.g. 2000)" to make it 
  immediately clear what is expected

#### Goal Setter Issues

**Issue 3:**
- Issue: Users wanted more guidance on what to enter in the 
  "Target" field on the goal creation form
- Hypothesis: The label "Target" is too vague — users don't know 
  if it means a number of miles, calories, minutes, or something else
- Remedy: Add placeholder text inside the Target field such as 
  "e.g. 10 miles" or "e.g. 500 calories" based on the selected 
  workout type

**Issue 4:**
- Issue: Users could not tell what workout the progress bar on the 
  Progress Tracker screen was tracking since the "Workout:" field 
  appeared blank
- Hypothesis: The progress tracker does not clearly show which 
  specific goal it is displaying, making it hard to understand 
  what 75% refers to
- Remedy: Pre-fill the Workout label with the goal name set during 
  goal creation, and add a subtitle such as 
  "Goal: Run 10 miles | Progress: 7.5 miles completed"

### Summary

Both participants were able to complete the tasks, but Participant 2 
highlighted a key theme: the app looks great visually but needs 
better guidance to help users understand what each element does. 
The main areas for improvement are:

1. Clearer labels and placeholder text in both features
2. Better explanation of interactive elements like the dropdown arrow
3. More context on the Progress Tracker so users know what they 
   are tracking

These fixes are low effort but would significantly improve how 
easily new users can understand and use the app.
